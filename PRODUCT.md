# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Three audiences, one site. **Prospective members** — adults 50 and better in the Hampton Roads area who are curious, active, and looking for engaging, low-pressure learning opportunities. **Current members** — returning to browse courses, register for new sessions, and stay connected with the community. **Donors and supporters** — individuals and families who want to sustain OLLI's mission through giving.

## Product Purpose

OLLI at Hampton University brings the Bernard Osher Foundation's national lifelong learning network to Hampton Roads. It exists to offer affordable, accessible, non-credit courses for adults 50 and better — no grades, no tests, no homework — in a member-led, community-driven environment that makes learning itself the reward. Success means more members enrolled, more courses offered, stronger donor support, and a growing sense of community among participants.

## Positioning

Hampton Roads' community for curious adults 50+ — member-led learning that's affordable, accessible, and built around the joy of discovery.

## Conversion & proof

- **Primary CTA:** Explore the course catalog. The visitor's first step is browsing what's available — seeing real courses builds interest and momentum.
- **Secondary CTA:** Become a member (annual membership registration). For visitors ready to commit.
- **The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds:** "Curiosity Never Retires."
- **Belief ladder:** OLLI exists → it's for people like me (50+, active) → it's affordable and easy → courses are genuinely interesting → I can join today.
- **Proof on hand:** Testimonials from members (featured on homepage); affiliation with the Bernard Osher Foundation and its network of 120+ institutes nationwide; partnership with Hampton University; real course catalog with photos; inaugural donor list.

## Brand Personality

Vibrant and energetic — OLLI is about active engagement, not passive retirement. The brand should feel warm, welcoming, and full of possibility. It respects the maturity of its audience without ever feeling staid or clinical. Tone is conversational, confident, and human — never academic jargon or institutional formality.

## Anti-references

Must not look like a generic university template — no dense academic layouts, no formal institutional styling, no clip-art or stock-photo fatigue. Should also avoid any aesthetic that reads as "designed for seniors" (oversaturated accessibility cues, medical-office tones, or anything that centers decline rather than vitality).

## Design Principles

1. **Curiosity leads.** Every page should make the visitor want to explore one step further — more courses, more activities, more reasons to join.
2. **Warmth without warmth-by-default.** The brand's navy-and-blue palette is confident and collegiate, not beige or cream. Warmth comes from photography, testimonials, and typography — not from a tinted background.
3. **Make it feel real.** Real people, real courses, real community. Photography of actual activities, genuine testimonials, and concrete course listings matter more than decorative patterns or abstract illustrations.
4. **Effortless readability.** The primary audience is 50+. Type is generously sized, line lengths are controlled, contrast is verified. Interaction targets are large enough for any hand.
5. **Practice what you preach.** The site itself should feel like a joy to explore — responsive, fast, delightful in small ways — because that's the experience OLLI promises.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Body text contrast of at least 4.5:1 against backgrounds, large text at least 3:1. Reduced motion support is built in. Focus-visible outlines are present. No specific WCAG level has been mandated, but AA-equivalent practices are the baseline given the audience.
